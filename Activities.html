Tourism Activites

Any visitor will look for sites that are well-suited to certain sports, such as diving, ziplining, fishing, or rock climbing, and Sri Lanka is one of the best places to do all of these. These activities will provide us with new perspectives on Sri Lanka's many cultures and traditions, as well as its various approaches and styles.

Things to do in Sri Lanka
include climbing Sigiriya, strolling the beaches of Arugam Bay, visiting a casino in Colombo, visiting a turtle hatchery in Bentota, and more. Wildlife safaris, scuba diving in Hikkaduwa, snorkeling, kitesurfing in Kalpitiya, and many more activities are available.


Diving


The sea around Sri Lanka speaks of legends from the past, tales of regal naval trips, bandits and pirates, and explorers and spice merchants. These stories come to life as you dive into the warm waters and dive lower. The Indian Ocean's crystal clear waters, with a visibility of 20 meters (65 feet), are ideal for diving. Deep reefs and reef walls provide exciting adventures for scuba divers, with wrecks dating back to the 18th century littering the oceans.


Hiking


Take a strenuous hike high in the Knuckles Range's highland rainforest to view the important spice cardamom growing in the forest, rare orchids and birds, and the thrill of exploring places where few have gone before. Travel through almost undiscovered country in the southeast, past the renowned Yala National Park, where leopards prowl and elephants graze, past the bird-rich Kumana wetlands, and into a region where hundreds of destroyed temples, possibly even towns, await discovery in the trees.


Fishing


The great names in deep-water game fishing, such as shark, marlin, and tuna, may be found in Sri Lanka's deep oceans. Closer to shore, the calmer seas are teeming with a variety of species, and the welcoming local fisherman are eager to welcome guests on their daily or nightly expeditions off the coast. The ocean's aroma and the excitement of the pursuit are exciting as the waves crash against the sides of the boat and sea birds call over your head.
