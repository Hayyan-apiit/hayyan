Sinharaja Forest

Historical facts

The Rain Forest and a Natural Hotspot in the south west lowland wet zone of Sri Lanka's Sabaragamu and Southern provinces. The Sinharaja rain forest is the country's only remaining primary tropical rain forest. Its primary and secondary forest covers 11,000 hectares. This small strip of undulating ground is made up of a variety of hills and valleys. In Sinharaja Forest, and over 60% of the trees are native, and many of them are deemed rare. The reserve is home to a diverse range of unique wildlife, including about half of Sri Lanka's rare butterfly and mammal species, as well as numerous insects, reptiles, and odd amphibians.
